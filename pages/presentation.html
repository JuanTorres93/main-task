Main concepts of salsa dancing

  • Basic steps: The fundamental movements that form the foundation of salsa dancing, including forward and backward steps, side steps, and cross-body leads.
  • Partner work: The interaction between the lead and follow dancers involves communication through body language, connection, and timing. The lead initiates movements, while the follow responds and complements the lead's actions.
  • Turns and spins: These are more advanced movements that add flair and excitement to salsa dancing. They require coordination, balance, and practice to execute smoothly. Examples include:
    • Right turns
    • Left turns
    • Spins
  • Styling and musicality: These elements involve finding the beat, adding personal flair, interpreting the music, and expressing emotions through dance. Dancers can incorporate body rolls, hip movements, and other stylistic choices to enhance their performance.
