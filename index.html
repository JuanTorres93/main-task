Salsa dancing

Salsa is a popular form of social dance that originated in the Caribbean, particularly in Cuba and Puerto Rico. It is characterized by its energetic and rhythmic movements, often performed to salsa music, which combines elements of Afro-Cuban rhythms with jazz and other musical styles.

I enjoy salsa dancing because it allows me to express myself through movement, connect with others, and stay active while having fun. The main concepts of salsa dancing include the basic steps, partner work, and various turns and spins. The basic steps involve a combination of forward and backward movements, while partner work focuses on leading and following techniques.

Author: Juan Torres
